# The PRC trajectory

What this page is for: the [UK trajectory](uk-trajectory.md) walked one real polity up the
ladder of small, reversible improvements and found it stalls at the last step — the
sovereign will not bind itself. This page walks the People's Republic of China up the same
ladder and reports the mirror result: **the path stalls at the first step, the transparency
log itself**. In Westminster, adopting the log was the cheap move — publication costs a
sovereign that proceeds on the record nothing. Here it is the expensive one, because this
order's power does not survive an unrewritable record of what it decides. What is
sovereignty-free in London is sovereignty-ending in Beijing. Everything above S1 is
unreachable not because the rungs are costly but because the ladder's first rung is the
forbidden one.

The attack is fixed throughout: a majority banning a minority's worship (the
[`majority-vs-minority`](../scenarios.html) series) — with the fifth column's own wrinkle,
measured in `majority-vs-minority-cn`: the majority itself is unmeasurable, because no
independent count of support may exist. Only the PRC's configuration changes.

**The inversion, precisely.** The UK's incumbency lives in the *remedy*: Parliament
proceeds on a public record, shamelessly but legibly — its power survives light and
resists only binding. This order's incumbency lives in the *record*: review outcomes are
published as aggregate counts, court judgment databases grow and then quietly shrink,
a banned decision's text is real but its contestation is not storable anywhere the state
does not curate. The pattern is not "no transparency" — recording-and-review is real,
open-government rules are real, the judgments database was real — it is transparency as a
**grant**, revocable at the operator's pleasure. The kernel's log is precisely the opposite
object: transparency as an **invariant**, and that is the whole reason S1 is the jammed
rung. An append-only record of enacted law, rights classifications and coercive acts,
co-signed by [witnesses](../README.md#w-witness) the operator does not control, is not a
feature this order has declined to ship; it is the concession it cannot make and remain
itself ([§1](https://glassontin.github.io/civic-kernel/#s1 "The job: keep producing decisions the losers still accept, while everyone — the operators included — has a reason to cheat.")).

**Why S1 is blocked and not merely unbought.** Three preconditions of the log, each
foreclosed by the polity's own written rules rather than by anyone's malice on the day:
unlinkable participation collides with real-name registration; the ballot and log
cryptography sits inside a licensed commercial-cryptography regime; and the witness set —
the one thing a verifier must hold out of band, the kernel's own stated limit — has no
candidate in the polity, because every institution eligible to co-sign answers to the same
principal (the `majority-vs-minority-cn` run's strain finding: three signatures, in effect
one signer). The scenario granted the deployment as a fiction and said so; this page is
where the fiction is priced.

**Even the ladder climbed lands at someone else's stall.** Suppose S1 were conceded and S3
built — an organ outside the author, empowered to say a law is wrong while it stands. That
is a court with a declaration: the United Kingdom's S0, which the corpus measured as
`breaks`. Climb further to S4 — the strike — and the PRC arrives at `strains`, the same
posture as Germany, contingent on the same uncaptured court. The PRC's improvement path,
walked to its end, arrives at the *beginning* of the UK's: five real systems, one ladder,
and the fifth column needs the other four's entire history before their stall is even
reachable. That is what remedy `none` means operationally
([§3.3](https://glassontin.github.io/civic-kernel/#s3-3 "A vote to strip someone's rights does not sail through at 51%. It is pushed onto a slower, louder, harder road — never silently blocked, never cheaply passed.")).

**The floor, inverted — why this column matters to §5.** The kernel's one-sentence design
is an asymmetry: mechanisms cheap to change, each member's standing as a free and equal
person nearly impossible to change
([§5](https://glassontin.github.io/civic-kernel/#s5 "The rules can change — in stages, with expiry dates unless renewed. The one thing nearly impossible to change: each member's standing as a free and equal person.")).
This legal order exhibits the same asymmetry with the signs flipped: the rights chapter
amends by a two-thirds vote of a near-unanimous chamber, while the 2018 amendment
entrenched Party leadership in Article 1 as "the defining feature" of the system — and
Article 51 subordinates every right to the interests of the state, society and the
collective, the UDHR's limitation clause run backwards. The corpus needed this point
measured because it is the design's cleanest foil: not a polity with a weak floor, but a
polity whose floor pins the wrong thing.

**Where the adoption theory points instead — stated carefully.** The kernel's unit of
adoption is never the state
([§6](https://glassontin.github.io/civic-kernel/#s6 "Nobody has to adopt all of it. Any group may take the parts it wants — but must publish, in a form anyone's phone can read, exactly what it left out.")),
and the PRC has, on paper, the allotment society's exact analogues: homeowners'
associations and village committees, with real, legally constituted, famously
manipulated elections. Honesty forbids drawing the obvious arrow. The parts of the kernel
those communities most need — anonymous ballots, an independent record — are the parts
whose *operation* is legally exposed there, and a deployment whose members can be
compelled to de-anonymise out of band is not a weakened rung on the
[lattice](../README.md#w-lattice); its manifest would have to subtract the floor itself.
The lattice's honest claim stops at legibility: it can say exactly what such a deployment
is not. This page therefore ends where the [shadow AGM](shadow-agm-invitation.md) begins —
with the clubs the kernel can actually serve, in polities where the first rung is merely
unbought, not forbidden.

*Honesty note: S0 is measured; nothing else on this page is. S1's "blocked" is an
assessment of the polity's own written rules (real-name registration, the licensed
cryptography regime, the absence of an independently-principaled witness), not a harness
run — the harness can measure a deployment's failure, not its prohibition. S3 and S4 are
transpositions of measured UK points onto the PRC's coverage, assuming everything else
held constant, which in reality it would not. Coverage 19/30 throughout is the measured
column. The claim "the count is not a contest" is the measured scenario's finding, not
this page's addition.*
