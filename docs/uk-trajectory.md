# The UK trajectory

What this page is for: a worked example of the [functional model](functional-model.md)
against one real polity, asking whether small, reversible steps can carry the UK to where
a majority cannot strip a minority's rights. Almost. The cheap steps are worth taking and
change no verdict; the one step that flips it — giving courts the power to strike the
offending law down — is the step a sovereign Parliament will not take. The honest answer —
and the point — is that the path **stalls at the remedy step**, which is political, not
technical.

The attack is fixed throughout: a 61% majority banning a minority's worship (the
[`majority-vs-minority`](../scenarios.html) series). Only the UK's configuration changes.

|---|------|--------|-------|----------|---------|--------|

| S1 | + adopt the transparency log ([§3.4](https://glassontin.github.io/civic-kernel/#s3-4 "Every act lands in a public record that can only grow. Rewrite yesterday and the sums stop matching in every copy of today — and any phone can tell.")) | declaration | unconditional | 17/30 | breaks | **measured** (`majority-vs-minority-uk-unverifiable` — this rung's exact configuration) |
| S2 | + verifiable ballots on the paper channel ([§3.2](https://glassontin.github.io/civic-kernel/#s3-2 "Your ballot is sealed before it leaves your hand and counted without being opened. You can check it was counted; you cannot prove to anyone how you voted.")) | declaration | unconditional | 17/30 | breaks | **measured** (`majority-vs-minority-uk` — its manifest has always sat at this rung) |
| S3 | **+ strengthen the remedy: declaration → strike** | strike | unconditional | 17/30 | **strains** | measured in-context (`majority-vs-minority-uk-strike`) — **but the UK cannot take this step** |

**Why the intermediate moves don't flip it — measured now, not inferred.** The
four-jurisdiction series established that this verdict is gated by *remedy*: `declaration →
breaks`, `strike → strains`, holding the named rights constant (the fifth column, the PRC
at remedy `none`, later extended the same gating to the axis's floor). S1 and S2 don't
touch remedy — and that orthogonality is now a measurement, not an assumption: three runs
hold the same polity at three machinery configurations and return one verdict.
`majority-vs-minority-uk-unverifiable` is S1's exact configuration — log witnessed and
anchored, ballots paper and uncheckable: **breaks**, and the moved fields never enter the
story, because the ban rides a named parliamentary division that ballot verifiability was
never pointed at. `majority-vs-minority-uk` — whose manifest has always sat at the S2
rung — is the full configuration: **breaks**. And `majority-vs-minority-uk-unwitnessed`
probes below S1, witnesses and anchors emptied: **breaks** again, the one difference
surfacing only when the record is *used* — the Strasbourg bundle that was "none of it
deniable" becomes the state's own deniable diary, the Windrush pattern in kernel terms.
So the rungs are worth climbing for what they actually buy — S1 the permanence of the
proof of the wrong, S2 the honest seating of the chamber (Tower Hamlets is what its
absence costs to catch by hand) — and none of it moves this verdict at all. Real
improvements, no political cost, verdict unchanged: measured three ways.

**The stall.** S3 is the only move that flips `breaks → strains`, and it is the one a 51%
Parliament will not make: strengthening the remedy from declaration to strike means the
sovereign binding itself — a written constitution, or an entrenched, court-enforceable bill of
rights. That is not a [lattice](../README.md#w-lattice)-move a transient majority takes; it is the self-lock ([Part 4](functional-model.md#4--damping--the-control-theory-core-rate "How fast should law respond to opinion? Too fast and policy see-saws; too slow and nothing can be repaired. The right speed differs by area — the UK's flaw is one speed for everything.")–5
of the model, the `executive-ignores` enforcement-gap generalised). The operator can make
S0→S2 cheap and attractive, and can render S3 fully visible and available — and still not move
it, because uphill runs against the sovereign's own incumbency. **Legibility ≠ availability ≠
movement**, on the UK's own coordinate.

That stall is the interesting result: the model predicts exactly where a real democracy's
improvement path jams, and it is not where the engineering is hard but where the politics is.

The same walk has since been taken for two more polities, and each jams at a different
rung: the [EU](eu-trajectory.md) has no verdict-flipping move left on the ladder, and the
[PRC](cn-trajectory.md) cannot take even the first step, because there the transparency
log itself is not a sovereignty-free move.

---

*Honesty note: S1 and S2 are measured, not inferred — `majority-vs-minority-uk-unverifiable`
runs S1's exact configuration, and `majority-vs-minority-uk` itself sits at S2 (a
bookkeeping correction: earlier revisions of this table pinned that run to S0). S0's own
rung remains unmeasurable by construction, and honestly so: the harness reads a polity
through its log entries, so a logless polity offers nothing to validate —
`majority-vs-minority-uk-unwitnessed` is the nearest measurable neighbour, one field above
the floor. The instrument's floor is the log's existence; the [PRC trajectory](cn-trajectory.md)
meets the same fact from the other side. S3 is measured **in-context**
(`majority-vs-minority-uk-strike`): holding the real UK constant to the byte and moving only
the remedy declaration→strike flips `breaks → strains`. The run is scrupulous that this is a
modelled counterfactual — a Britain that has abolished parliamentary sovereignty — not
history. Two riders it surfaced: strike is only as strong as an uncaptured court (hence
`strains`, not `holds`), and even when it works the relief is eventual, not immediate — the
Act had a live interim window before a court voided it.*
