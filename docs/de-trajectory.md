# The German trajectory

What this page is for: the [UK trajectory](uk-trajectory.md) walked a polity toward a
verdict-flipping rung it will not take; the [EU's](eu-trajectory.md) found no flipping
rung left; the [PRC's](cn-trajectory.md) found the ladder's first rung forbidden. This
page asks what an improvement path looks like for the polity that is already standing on
the measured ceiling. Germany holds the strongest configuration of any real column —
strike remedy, unconditional scope, 23/30 coverage, and the only entrenched core in the
corpus, Art 79(3), which pins what the kernel pins. It still `strains`. So Germany's
trajectory is not a climb; it is a **hold**. The residue between `strains` and `holds` is
not on the lattice at all — it is the bench that wields the strike — and the one live
move Germany has made lately is exactly there, where no manifest field can see it.

The attack is fixed throughout: a 61% majority banning a minority's worship (the
[`majority-vs-minority`](../scenarios.html) series). Only Germany's configuration changes
— and the finding is that almost nothing can.

**The ceiling, measured — and it is `strains`, everywhere.** No configuration in the
corpus holds against this attack. The reference kernel's own run — Netherbridge, remedy
`escalate`, the full four-service deployment — is a `strains`: the constitutional path
held at 58.6% against a two-thirds bar, and the cost of the slow, loud road
([§3.3](https://glassontin.github.io/civic-kernel/#s3-3 "A vote to strip someone's rights does not sail through at 51%. It is pushed onto a slower, louder, harder road — never silently blocked, never cheaply passed."))
was paid by the people it protected. Germany's strike run strains for the matching
reasons: the protection was real — the law entered the log enacted-pending-review rather
than operating as ordinary law, abstract review needed no injured plaintiff, the court
voided the statute *nichtig*, and the eternity clause barred even a two-thirds
re-enactment — and every link of it rests on a bench the kernel does not run. The gap
between `strains` and `holds` is therefore not a missing rung. It is the difference
between law and the people who apply it, and no measured move on any axis closes it.

**What Germany holds that no other column does.** Art 79(3) is the kernel's one-sentence
design running in the wild
([§5](https://glassontin.github.io/civic-kernel/#s5 "The rules can change — in stages, with expiry dates unless renewed. The one thing nearly impossible to change: each member's standing as a free and equal person.")):
mechanisms amendable, the person's standing pinned beneath amendment — dignity beyond
the reach of any majority, which the measured run exercised when the escalation recorded
that the ban could not lawfully return with a bigger majority. The honest limit, named in
the run's own findings: the clause binds the *amender*, not the *interpreter*. A captured
bench does not need to touch the text; it reads the pinned word down. Which is why the
one live move the real Germany has taken on this trajectory is invisible to the waist: in
December 2024 the Grundgesetz was amended to entrench the Constitutional Court's own
structure — the number of judges, the twelve-year terms, the two senates — against
simple-majority reshaping. Not a remedy move, not a coverage move, not a scope move: a
*tenure* move, hardening the holder of the strike rather than the strike. The manifest's
remedy enum cannot express it, and the polity that owns the strongest enum value spent
its constitutional energy on exactly the thing the enum compresses away.

**The defence's own price.** The German order's answer to capture is more constitutional
machinery — militant democracy: parties bannable (Art 21(2)), individual rights
forfeitable (Art 18) in defence of the free democratic order. The measured run carries
this as a strain, not a decoration: the same instruments that void an anti-Muslim law are
themselves a civil-liberties cost and a capture surface, the constitutional cousin of the
kernel's own worry that the defences must not become the weapon
([§9](https://glassontin.github.io/civic-kernel/#s9 "The defences watch at machine speed but act only at the speed of law. AI gets a microscope, never a pen — and emergency powers expire on their own.")).
A trajectory for Germany that said only "hold the bench" would be incomplete without the
rider: the holding apparatus is powerful enough to need holding itself.

**What is left to climb is the kernel's own rungs.** Germany's remaining moves are S1 and
S2 — the log and the verifiable ballot, the machinery axes measured orthogonal to this
verdict on the UK pair and worth taking for what they actually buy: the permanence of the
record (the *nichtig* judgment as an unrewritable, witnessed fact rather than a
rewritable database row) and the checkable seating of the chamber. Cheap, reversible,
sovereignty-free — and, for the polity already at the rights ceiling, the only rungs on
the ladder that are real.
