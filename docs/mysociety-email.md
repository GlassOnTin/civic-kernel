**Subject:** WhatDoTheyKnow and FixMyStreet are halfway through two findings a
sixty-one-case corpus can now cite

Dear [name — or "the mySociety team"],

The attached page makes one claim you are unusually well placed to test: two of your
tools already embody half of two findings from a study of sixty-one documented
institutional failures, each rebuilt as a machine-checked case file.

For WhatDoTheyKnow: the FOI case file concludes that every slow-walking lever — the
missed deadline, the stacked extension, the profiled requester — acts on the
*request*, so the end-state your platform points at is a record public by default,
with nothing left to request. For FixMyStreet: two case files, Grenfell's ignored
hazard reports and SEND assessments rationed by delay, reduce to the shape your
platform half-builds already — the public, dated report, still waiting for its other
half: the duty-clock, and a default on breach.

The ask is a look and corrections, especially from your research side. The case files
are public data; where the encoding is wrong, saying so is as valuable as agreeing,
and the door is open either way.

With admiration for twenty years of building the citizen's half,

[Ian Williams]
